## Want to know more?

Before moving into tech, I worked for a decade in the specialty coffee industry as a trainer and educator. I went to University of Michigan on a swimming scholarship (500 and 1,650 freestyle were my events), but eventually dropped out of school for personal reasons. I love mentoring other developers from non-traditional backgrounds as I do not come from a CS background and am self taught in mobile development. While my bread and butter has been iOS (Swift/Objective-C) I have also built a number of features on Android (Kotlin/Java) and enjoy working cross platform. Specifically, I love all things SDKs - buiding them, mainining them, supporting them, weird package manager intricacies, all of it.

My wife and I own a 1920s dutch colonial revival home in Oak Park, IL that we are lovingly renovating/restoring mostly ourselves. While most of the big projects are done (hello only full bathroom renovation after we moved in, entire basement during covid with a 1 year old at home, buying a tile saw, etc) we've got a long list of other projects we are slowly knocking out. We have a daughter (7) as well as 1 dog (Sadie) and 2 cats (Wednesday and Pugsley). When we aren't working on our house, we can be found outside - but not camping, walking around Oak Park admiring the Frank Lloyd Wright houses, visiting museums, hanging out with friends, at Disneyland (way too often), traveling, making homemade pizza (almost every Friday), or eating dessert.

## When and How I Work

Typically 8am - 4pm Central. I tend to do my deepest work in the morning and enjoy starting with a high level of focus right after our daughter goes to school/camp, depending on the time of year. I typically line my time off up with when our daughter is out of school, which typically means 1-2 weeks off a quarter to reset. I also believe everyone should have their birthday off work! In the summer I sometimes start later to hit the gym in the morning since I mostly wake up with the sun. In the winter, I tend to take a longer lunch to go to the gym then instead and make up time either earlier or later in the day.
